Alexa Skill Tree

Overview

The Alexa Skill Tree project was a game developed while three members of Apartment 401 interned at Amazon. Alexa Skill Tree was submitted to the Amazon Interns Hackathon in Summer 2020 and was built by Warren Gu, Felix Lin, and Paul Ngo.

Goal

We wanted to create a gamified conversation with Alexa to provide an onboarding experience for future Amazon interns.

Solution

We did research in order to identify what interns thought about the current onboarding process. In particular, we aimed to identify things interns liked and pain points interns had with the current onboarding process. Our goal was to use these insights to develop ideas for what would be most helpful to interns in a pre-onboarding experience.

Some key points we found were that:

  1. The Embark onboarding experience seemed lengthy and monotonous at some points to a few interns.
  2. Many found that they needed additional support during onboarding to track their IT equipment and troubleshoot IT issues
  3. Some interns expressed interest in learning more about Amazon and their specific teams prior to beginning the internship.

Looking at the key insights we arrived at, we were able to narrow down the scope of our project to one primary focus: How might we build an Alexa Skill to create an engaging experience that would introduce new interns to Amazon and their place within Amazon, while also providing them adequate support to begin their internship successfully?
Because of Alexa's nature as a primarily voice based application, we did not want to create an extensive mobile application interface for this Alexa Skill and instead just focus on its voice-related capabilities. We felt that would take away from the identity of this pre-onboarding experience as an Alexa Skill and instead turn it into something more like an Amazon pre-onboarding app.

We created an Alexa skill using the Alexa Developer Console, and developed a skill tree which users of the skill could navigate by reading material and answering questions.
